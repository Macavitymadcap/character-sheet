## Backstory

I grew up in the factory district of Rovnost, part of a long line of hobgoblin engineers. My family worked the machines that kept the city running. My father, his father, generations feeding the furnaces and maintaining the great industrial engines. I had the family gift for tinkering, for understanding how things worked, but I also had ambition. When the military began recruiting technically skilled individuals for their new magitech weapons program, I saw my opportunity: escape the smog, see the world, make something of myself beyond the factory floor.

I excelled in the 1st Astrilian Artificers. The discipline came naturally; hobgoblin culture had prepared me for military life. The unit wasn't just about firepower. We were infiltrators, intelligence gatherers, saboteurs with experimental magitech at our disposal. I learned to move unseen through urban terrain, to blend into crowds, to gather information and disappear before anyone noticed. I became proficient with experimental magitech firearms, rose through the ranks to Corporal, and earned respect as both a soldier and a technician. We operated in the shadows, maintaining order through precision strikes and covert operations. The work was dangerous, sometimes morally grey, but I told myself I was protecting progress, maintaining stability. The doubts were there. Whispers in the back of my mind during certain operations. But the alternative was going back to the factories, so I pushed them down and did my duty.

Until they sent me home.

The order came down: suppress a workers' uprising in my old district. Use the new weapon, an experimental magitech cannon designed for "crowd control with minimal casualties." Sergeant Kora Steelheart gave the order without hesitation. We were assured it was humane, effective, necessary. Private First Class Rennik Coppergear had made sure the cannon worked perfectly. I carried out the mission.

The cannon worked exactly as designed. Alchemically enhanced shot spread over distance, rupturing bodies from within. Turning people inside out without fully killing them. Leaving them dying in agony on the factory floor. I killed people I knew. I heard Riggo "Three-Finger" Tarn screaming; we'd grown up together, and he'd been organizing the workers. I saw Old Korrin in the crowd before I fired; he'd taught me everything I knew about engineering. Childhood friends. Neighbors. Maybe even my Aunt Marta, though I couldn't bring myself to look closely enough to know for sure. Familiar faces now screaming, their insides on the outside, begging for death that wouldn't come quickly.

Corporal "Matchstick" Venn was sick in the alley afterward, but he stayed. He believed the system could be fixed from within. Private Dulsa Ironbrace deserted three days after me; I don't know if she made it out. Others, like Sergeant Steelheart, believed it was necessary, righteous even. These were traitors threatening the city's prosperity, they said. Captain Theron Blackwood filed his after-action report praising the mission's success. Major Selvanis Kresh, who'd briefed us on the weapon's "minimal casualties," knew exactly what it would do all along.

I couldn't reconcile it anymore. The doubts became certainties. Within days, I deserted.

I took my service weapons; a rifle and revolver, both heavily modified to be unrecognizable. I learned minor illusion magic, enough to hide the scars crossing my face and the regimental tattoo marking my shoulder. Now I'm back where I started: the factory district, blood on my hands, maintaining weapons I resent out of pure habit, wearing borrowed faces and hoping nobody looks too closely at who I really am.

## Key NPCs

### The 1st Astrilian Artificers

#### Sergeant Kora Steelheart
**Role:** Female Hobgoblin, Squad Leader

She gave the order to fire that night and would do it again. Kora saw the uprising as a threat to stability, and stability is everything. She's likely leading the search for me now, not out of personal vendetta, but because desertion undermines discipline. Methodical, respected, utterly convinced of the righteousness of her actions.

#### Corporal "Matchstick" Venn Ashlock
**Role:** Male Human, Demolitions Specialist

He was sick in the alley afterward, but he didn't leave. Venn hated what happened but believes the system can be fixed from within. I've heard rumors he's been reassigned to training duty, kept away from field operations. Part of me wonders if he thinks about that night as much as I do.

#### Private Dulsa Ironbrace
**Role:** Female Dwarf, Sharpshooter

She deserted three days after I did. I don't know where she went or if she's still alive. During the mission she followed orders without hesitation, but I saw something break in her eyes when the screaming started. If she's out there, she's either dead, caught, or hiding just as desperately as I am.

#### Private First Class Rennik Coppergear
**Role:** Male Gnome, Magitech Engineer

The one who maintained the experimental cannon. Rennik was excited about the technology, fascinated by its efficiency. He didn't fire it; he just made sure it worked perfectly. I wonder if that distinction helps him sleep at night. He's probably still with the unit, probably still tinkering.

### Command Structure

#### Captain Theron Blackwood
**Role:** Male Human, Company Commander

Career officer. Gave the orders with complete confidence, filed the after-action report praising the mission's success. To him, I'm not a deserter; I'm a malfunctioning asset. Cold, competent, and politically connected. If anyone's coordinating the search for me, it's Blackwood.

#### Major Selvanis Kresh
**Role:** Non-binary Half-Elf, Intelligence Officer

The one who briefed us on the "minimal casualties" nature of the new weapon. They knew exactly what it would do. I'm certain of that now. Selvanis is a true architect of the system, someone who sees people as variables in an equation. Dangerous and untouchable.

### People From Home

#### Marta Magulbisson
**Relation:** Female Hobgoblin, My Aunt

She worked the steam lines at the factory where the uprising happened. I don't know if she was there that night. I don't know if she survived. I haven't dared to check.

#### Riggo "Three-Finger" Tarn
**Relation:** Male Half-Orc, Childhood Friend

He lost two fingers to a machine accident when we were both twelve. He used to joke that the factory would take the rest of him piece by piece. He was one of the uprising's organizers. I recognized his voice among the screaming before it stopped.

#### Old Korrin
**Relation:** Male Hobgoblin, Master Engineer, My Mentor

He taught me and my father both. Believed engineering was about building a better world. He was too old to fight, but he supported the workers' demands. I saw him in the crowd before I fired. I don't know what happened to him after.

## Military Rank Structure - 1st Astrilian Artificers

### Enlisted Ranks (Technical Specialists)
- **Private (Pvt)** - Entry level, basic training complete
- **Private First Class (PFC)** - Proven competent, trusted with specialized equipment
- **Corporal (Cpl)** - Junior leadership, fire team leader, technical specialist
- **Sergeant (Sgt)** - Squad leader, veteran operator

### Officer Ranks (Command)
- **Lieutenant (Lt)** - Platoon leader, 2-4 squads
- **Captain (Capt)** - Company commander, multiple platoons
- **Major (Maj)** - Battalion staff, intelligence/operations officer
